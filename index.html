Demand Elasticity and Product Substitutability of U.S. TV Advertising

Undergraduate Senior Thesis.
Initial version: May 2019. Revised: September 2020

Abstract: I study the demand elasticity and product substitution patterns in the U.S. television advertising market. (More...)

Abstract: I study the demand elasticity and product substitution patterns in the U.S. television advertising market. Relying on the nested logit model of demand, I utilize an extensive dataset on the U.S. prime time TV advertising market to estimate the price elasticity of demand and uncover the substitution pattern of products in this market. My baseline specification suggests that the demand elasticity parameter is -0.42 for the 2011-2012 season, and -0.88 for the 2012-2013 season. For the advertisers, telecasts provided by the same conglomerate are highly substitutable to each other relative to out-of-conglomerate telecasts. This implies that increasing the variety of telecasts will not attract many new advertisers, which could discourage the conglomerates from increasing their product diversity. (...Less)

Impact of Retirement on Healthcare Utilization and Health Outcomes

Class Paper for Applied Health Economics
Initial version: May 2019

Abstract: We study the relationship between retirement and healthcare utilization and health outcomes. (More...)

Abstract: We study the relationship between retirement and healthcare utilization and health outcomes. The lifestyle transition to retirement may incur changes in health outcomes, and more generous medical plans included in retirement benefits might encourage people to expand their healthcare expenditure. In this paper, we explore the aggregate effects of retirement on health outcomes and healthcare expenditure through both channels. Our empirical challenge is to account for adverse selection in retirement decisions - those who are less healthy are more inclined to retire. Exploiting the discontinuity in percentage of Irish who retire at age 66, we use an instrumental variable approach to implement a fuzzy regression discontinuity design on aging data in Ireland. We find no conclusive evidence of significant effects of retirement on various health outcomes and healthcare utilization measures. (...Less)

Projects

COVID-19 Data Imputation

This project aims to use K-nearest-neighbors (KNN) algorithm to impute the "missing data" in the early stage of COVID-19, when both public awareness and testing capacities were insufficient.

More specifically, we collect the John Hopkins Coronavirus Resource Center daily reported cumulative confirmed cases data from 266 country/regions or their state/provinces on the world. We use the KNN method to compare the case development histories of different locations and match locations with their nearest neighbors on the cumulative cases graph in the long run , to infer the actual numbers of COVID-19 cases in the short run when the data seem to be missing there
